Estimating Groundwater Recharge with Remote Sensing

Identifying spatial recharge zones using satellite data and water balance modeling.

Overview

Remote sensing offers a low-cost, scalable method to estimate groundwater recharge potential in regions with limited monitoring infrastructure. By combining satellite-derived evapotranspiration, precipitation, and land cover data, we can infer recharge variability across space and time.

Methodology

  • Precipitation data from CHIRPS or TRMM
  • Evapotranspiration from MODIS or SSEBop
  • NDVI and land use classification
  • Recharge estimated as P - ET for select windows

Python tools (xarray, GEE, geopandas) support processing and visualization.

Outcomes

These maps help prioritize managed aquifer recharge (MAR) zones, validate hydrological models, or inform land-use planning. They also form part of participatory groundwater management with communities and NGOs.
